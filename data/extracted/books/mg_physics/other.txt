Page 143: The section “Elastic and Inelastic Collision” is not meant to imply that momentum
conservation can only be applied to perfectly elastic or inelastic collisions. The conservation of
momentum holds for all collisions. A perfectly inelastic or elastic collision simply makes our
calculations simpler.


It can be difficult to solve conservation of momentum problems if both final velocities are
unknown. However, if you know one final velocity or know the objects stick together (perfectly
inelastic collision), you can solve for the other final velocity.

φ (phi), not q and f.

zero means that the momentum does not change.”

height transforms into kinetic energy in a falling object.”

“braking” on this page.
